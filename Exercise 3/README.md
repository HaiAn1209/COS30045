# COS30045 – Exercise 3: TV Energy Consumption Data Story

## Data Story

### Audience
The intended audience for this visualisation is Australian household consumers who are interested
in purchasing a television and are concerned about energy consumption and electricity costs.

### Audience Needs
The audience wants to understand:
- Which TV technologies are most common in Australia
- Whether larger TVs consume more power
- Which screen technologies are more energy efficient

### Storytelling Approach
The website follows a clear narrative:
1. Introduce the TV market context
2. Explore the relationship between screen size and power use
3. Compare energy efficiency across screen technologies
4. Conclude with practical insights for consumers

## About the Data

### Data Source
The dataset contains information about television models available in Australia, including screen
technology, screen size, and power consumption.

### Data Processing
The data was cleaned and aggregated using KNIME in Exercise 2. Aggregated tables were used to
generate the charts presented in this website.

### Privacy
The dataset contains no personal or identifiable information.

### Accuracy and Limitations
The data represents a snapshot of available TV models and may not fully represent the entire
Australian market. Power consumption values are manufacturer-reported.

### Ethics
The visualisations are designed to inform consumers and avoid misleading representations.

GenAI tools were used to assist with structuring the data story and drafting initial content.
All generated material was reviewed, edited, and fully understood by the author.
