TV Energy Consumption in Australia

A data story about how screen technology and size affect electricity use

Televisions are one of the most common electrical appliances in Australian households. With rising electricity prices, understanding how different TVs consume energy can help consumers make more informed purchasing decisions.

What TV screen technologies are most common?

Understanding which screen technologies are most common provides context for the rest of the story.

LED-based televisions dominate the Australian market, while OLED models are less common.

Does screen size affect power consumption?

Screen size is a key factor when purchasing a TV. Larger screens may consume more electricity.

Larger TVs generally consume more power, although efficiency varies between models.

Which screen technology is the most energy efficient?

Comparing average power consumption across screen technologies helps identify more energy-efficient options.

On average, newer screen technologies tend to consume less power.

Conclusion

Both screen size and screen technology influence TV energy consumption. Consumers should consider long-term electricity costs, not just price and size.
